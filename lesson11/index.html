Latest Forecast

Posted By: Nate Larsen - Morning Meteorologist Updated: Friday, March 6, 2020, at 7:45 a.m.

Another day of breezy winds and above normal temperatures. Highs on Friday are expected to reach the upper 60's, near or above records for this time of year. Mild temperatures are due to the increasing southwest flow across the region driven by an approaching storm system. A cold front is expected to move through on Saturday afternoon, some valley rain and mountain rain/snow showers are expected. Also, I can't rule out an isolated thunderstorm or two in parts of the valley with the arrival of the cold front. Snow levels will likely hover around 5,000 feet on Saturday. More seasonal temperatures and partly to mostly cloudy skies are anticipated early next week.

Snowstorm Expected to Make Roads Dangerous

Drivers can expect high winds, blowing and drifting snow, low visitibllity and heavey snow in higher elevationns. Up to five inches of snow in the valley and nine inches in the mountains is expected over the next 24 hours, in Franklin County, the eastern part of Oneida county and north thoughout the Snake River Plain. The Idaho State Polic and ITD are encouraging motorists to use caution if they must travel.
